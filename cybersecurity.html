Identifying Social Engineering Attacks

Home | Case Studies | Cybersecurity | Reflection
Have you ever gotten an extremely suspicious email, phone text, or call, and do not know if it is not a scam? After reading this webpage, you will become an expert at detecting them and other forms of what is called social engineering: a tactic used by cybercriminals to trick people into giving away personal information. Here, you'll learn how to spot the signs of social engineering attacks, understand the various techniques used by scammers, and get tips on protecting yourself and your information. Stay aware, stay safe, and fight the phish!

How to identify each type of social engineering:

Phishing Smishing Vishing Pharming
Check the sender's email address carefully for slight misspellings or domain changes Be suspicious of unsolicited messages asking for personal information or offering something that seems too good to be true Be cautious of numbers that appear to be from legitimate organizations but request sensitive information Always check the URL for slight misspellings or unusual characters
Look for poor grammar and spelling mistakes Avoid clicking on links in text messages from unknown numbers Legitimate organizations typically don't ask for personal information over the phone Look for HTTPS and a padlock symbol in the browser's address bar (secure connection)
Hover over links to see the actual URL before clicking Contact the supposed sender through official channels to verify the legitimacy of the message Hang up and call the official number of the institution to verify the call's authenticity Use security software that provides DNS protection

After learning about social engineering and how to identify the different types, you should now be a master at detecting fake emails, messages, and phone calls.
